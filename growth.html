Strategic Intent

Why This Enabler Exists

Wellabe has momentum. Since 2021 — policy count up 20%, revenue up 24%, STP from 23% to 67%, ancillary sales growing. The demographic tailwind is real: the silver tsunami and a growing Medicare Supplement market create a window that won't stay open indefinitely. The question is not whether to grow, but where to double down and where to stop subsidizing underperformance.

Growth is not a blanket directive. It is a portfolio of deliberate bets — each with a time horizon, a capital requirement, and a return threshold. Profitable growth in Medicare Supplement funds the platform investment that makes digital possible.

What It Is Meant To Solve

  • →Over-reliance on traditional distribution channels with limited digital reach
  • →Underperforming Preneed distribution that dilutes margin without adding volume value
  • →Ancillary health sales that have not kept pace with the Medicare Supplement growth trajectory

Key Tradeoffs

  • →Medicare Supplement growth is capital-intensive. Aggressive sales scenario creates RBC pressure that requires external capital solutions.
  • →Preneed pruning reduces short-term revenue by ~18% to protect long-term margin quality
  • →Ancillary health growth requires digital investment before it generates scaled returns

What We Committed To

Strategic Objectives

Medicare Supplement

Top 5 carrier. 300K+ members. $200M annual sales by 2030.

60%+ underwriting mix, <24hr turnaround, digital-first enrollment. Reinsurance to manage capital strain while maintaining growth trajectory.

Ancillary Health

Grow ancillary health sales year-over-year. CI and HI as core products.

Hospital Indemnity and Critical Illness are the growth vehicles. Expand to younger demographics via digital. White-label opportunities with distribution partners. Growing alongside — not dependent on — Medicare Supplement sales.

Preneed

Digital leader serving a curated network of high-value partners.

Prune from 275 to 50 active agencies. Maintain VNB while reducing sales volume. Multi-pay mix to 50%. Concierge-level digital servicing experience for retained partners.

Platform as Product

MyEnroller4 as the digital distribution engine.

AI-guided enrollment, real-time agent assignment, API-ready for white-label. The platform is a competitive moat — not just a tool. 30% of MS apps via consumer-facing platform by 2030.

Talent

The right capabilities to execute at each phase.

Execution risk in growth is often a talent problem first. Digital UX for enrollment, data science for agent scoring, actuarial depth for product management — these are non-optional.

Agent Experience

Make Wellabe the easiest carrier to do business with.

Agent Portal MVP live June 2026. Real-time incentive capabilities. Performance dashboards. Agents who produce more with us stay with us — the experience is part of the growth strategy.

How This Shows Up Operationally

Phased Execution Roadmap

Growth is a phased portfolio — not a single initiative. Each phase has clear outcomes and capital implications.

Phase 1 — Launch & Focus

2025–2026
  • Accelerated underwriting — 24hr MS turnaround
  • CI Easy Button live; HI riders added in 10 states
  • Preneed: data-driven pruning from 275 to 50 agencies
  • Agent Portal MVP launched (June 2026)
  • Digital enrollment tooling for agents (MyEnroller4)

Phase 2 — Scale & Optimize

2026–2028
  • Consumer-facing MS enrollment (30% of apps)
  • Preneed: 75%+ digital adoption across remaining partners
  • Ancillary health sales system built into agent portal
  • White-label HI/CI opportunities for distribution partners
  • MS reinsurance (quota share) executed
  • Agent subscription model — platform as a product

Phase 3 — Own & Differentiate

2028–2030
  • Top 5 MS carrier with 300K+ members
  • 100% paperless Preneed operations
  • Ancillary health as a scaled standalone growth engine
  • Platform ecosystem enabling agent subscription model
  • Consumer-facing direct enrollment live and growing
  • White-label distribution at scale

Leadership Signals

What This Enabler Helps Leaders Answer

Questions This Enabler Answers

  • Are we capturing enough of the Medicare Supplement market before the competitive window narrows?
  • Is Preneed pruning on pace, and are we protecting VNB through the transition?
  • Is ancillary health sales growth accelerating as digital products come online?
  • Is agent experience improving fast enough to drive retention and production?

What Yellow/Red Signals Mean

Yellow on execution means we are behind on building the capability — usually a resources, priorities, or dependencies problem. Solvable in-quarter with management attention.

Yellow on outcomes means the market or product isn't performing as modeled. Requires a management response: repricing, distribution change, or assumption revision.

Red on growth KPIs is rare and serious. It means a strategic assumption has broken down and the plan needs a formal response — not just a quarterly note.

What Is Intentionally Not Optimized Here

Growth does not optimize for short-term sales volume at the expense of underwriting quality. A 40% UW mix at $200M revenue is worse than a 60% UW mix at $130M. Volume without quality destroys embedded value and creates future capital problems.
