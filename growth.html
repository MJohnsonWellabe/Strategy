Strategic Intent

Why This Enabler Exists

Wellabe has momentum. Since 2021 — policy count up 20%, revenue up 24%, STP from 23% to 67%, ancillary sales growing. The demographic tailwind is real: the silver tsunami, record annuity demand, a growing Medicare Supplement market. The question is not whether to grow, but where to double down and where to stop subsidizing underperformance.

Growth is not a blanket directive. It is a portfolio of deliberate bets — each with a time horizon, a capital requirement, and a return threshold. Profitable growth in Medicare Supplement funds the platform investment that makes digital possible.

What It Is Meant To Solve

  • →Over-reliance on traditional distribution channels with limited digital reach
  • →Underperforming Preneed distribution that dilutes margin without adding volume value
  • →No annuity revenue stream — a significant gap as Baby Boomers move to decumulation
  • →Ancillary health cross-sell at ~2% when the target is 5%

Key Tradeoffs

  • →Medicare Supplement growth is capital-intensive. Aggressive sales scenario exhausts Holdco.
  • →Preneed pruning reduces short-term revenue by ~18% to protect long-term margin quality
  • →Annuity growth depends on reinsurance partner execution — capital-light but counterparty-exposed

What We Committed To

Strategic Objectives

Medicare Supplement

Top 5 carrier. 300K+ members. $200M annual sales by 2030.

60%+ underwriting mix, <24hr turnaround, digital-first enrollment. IRR target 12%+ on new sales. Reinsurance to manage capital strain.

Ancillary Health

$30M+ in annual ancillary sales. Cross-sell from 2% to 5%.

Hospital Indemnity and Critical Illness as core products. Expansion to younger demographics via digital. White-label opportunities with distribution partners.

Preneed

Digital leader serving a curated network of high-value partners.

Prune from 275 to 50 active agencies. Maintain VNB while reducing sales volume. Multi-pay mix to 50%. Concierge-level digital servicing experience.

Annuity

MYGA launched. FIA by 2027. $2B+ AUM by 2030.

Capital-light via Aspida Re partnership. Expand to FIA with distribution partner capital commitments. IRR target ~12%. Retain increasing portion of risk over time.

Platform as Product

MyEnroller4 as the digital distribution engine.

AI-guided enrollment, real-time agent assignment, API-ready for white-label. The platform is a competitive moat — not just a tool. 30% of MS apps via consumer-facing platform by 2030.

Talent

The right capabilities to execute at each phase.

Execution risk in growth is often a talent problem first. ALM expertise for annuities, digital UX for enrollment, data science for agent scoring — these are non-optional.

How This Shows Up Operationally

Phased Execution Roadmap

Growth is a phased portfolio — not a single initiative. Each phase has clear outcomes and capital implications.

Phase 1 — Launch & Focus

2025–2026
  • Accelerated underwriting for 24hr MS turnaround
  • CI Easy Button live; HI riders added in 10 states
  • MYGA launched via Aspida Re capital-light structure
  • Preneed: data-driven pruning from 275 to 50 agencies
  • Agent Portal MVP launched (June 2026)
  • Digital enrollment tooling for agents (MyEnroller4)

Phase 2 — Scale & Optimize

2026–2028
  • FIA product launched with distribution partner sidecar
  • Consumer-facing MS enrollment (30% of apps)
  • Preneed: 75%+ digital adoption across remaining partners
  • Ancillary health cross-sell system built into portal
  • White-label HI/CI opportunities for distribution partners
  • MS reinsurance (C-2 financing + quota share) executed

Phase 3 — Own & Differentiate

2028–2030
  • Top 5 MS carrier with 300K+ members
  • FIA scaled to $400M+ in annual sales
  • Hybrid health/annuity products launched
  • 100% paperless Preneed operations
  • Decoupled from annuity launch partner; own ALM
  • Platform ecosystem with agent subscription model

Leadership Signals

What This Enabler Helps Leaders Answer

Questions This Enabler Answers

  • Are we capturing enough of the Medicare Supplement market before the competitive window narrows?
  • Is Preneed pruning on pace, and are we protecting VNB through the transition?
  • Are annuity products generating the scale needed for FIA to be viable?
  • Is ancillary health cross-sell moving from 2% toward the 5% target?

What Yellow/Red Signals Mean

Yellow on execution means we are behind on building the capability. This is usually a resources, priorities, or dependencies problem — solvable in-quarter.

Yellow on outcomes means the market or the product isn't performing as modeled. This requires a management response — repricing, distribution change, or assumption revision.

Red on growth KPIs is rare and serious. It means a strategic assumption has broken down and the plan needs a formal response.

What Is Intentionally Not Optimized Here

Growth does not optimize for short-term sales volume at the expense of underwriting quality. A 40% UW mix at $200M revenue is worse than a 60% UW mix at $130M. Volume without quality destroys embedded value.
