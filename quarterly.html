Q1 2026 Operating Memo

📈 Growth

Status
4 Green   2 Yellow   0 Red
What's Working
Medicare Supplement optimization is producing real value: $2M in YTD marketing allowance savings, $1.2M in incentive savings, 49% UW mix. Ancillary health sales up 20% in Q1 2026 vs. Q1 2025 — the CI Easy Button is in market and working. MS execution is ahead of plan on charter management (MO, CO, KS flipped) and weighted premium rerates (19%).
What's Yellow
Both Preneed (PN) KPIs are yellow. The PN quality score has not been implemented. No agents have been terminated yet. These actions are planned for coming months — the annual plan is in process. The PN outcome KPI (margin) reflects the transition period: multi-pay mix is improving but the full benefit of the pruning strategy won't show until Phase 2.
What's Next
Continue MS charter management — additional state rerate opportunities in pipeline. Advance ancillary health digital tooling. PN quality score and agent network refinements underway per annual plan timeline.

⚡ Digital-First

Status
23 Green   1 Yellow
What's Working
23 of 24 Digital KPIs are green — the strongest scorecard of any enabler. Data quality score at 95 (target >90). User engagement rate 50%, above goal. 333 of 374 employees actively using Copilot with 20,000 prompts in last 30 days. Agent Portal MVP on track for late June. Churn modeling begun — delivering Q2 with journeys starting Q3. Payment journeys on track for end of Q2.
What's Yellow
New Business automation is yellow at 1% increase vs. 7% target. Health volumes are below 2025 levels due to GI/SEP complexity and a slight increase in underwritten cases not going STP. Automation projects for GI/SEP Med Sup are in progress and expected to launch before AEP. Preneed continues to trend up. No intervention required at this stage.
What's Next
Agent Portal MVP launch (June 2026). AI intake round 1 (June 2026). Message Center delivery (July/August). CSAT program kickoff (July). Customer Portal requirements gathering ongoing.

🏛️ Capital

Status
4 Green   2 Red
What's Working
Capital framework is operational and was used for 26R2 planning. Actuarial modeling is on track — pre-covariance RBC within tolerance at ~2bps. Capital scenario modeling was completed in Q1 and is ready to be shared with CapCo. Cash flow forecasting model progressing with appropriate resources assigned.
What's Red
RBC trajectory: RBC projected to decline well below 450% — reaching approximately 350% at the mid-period low point at expected sales levels. The 450% target is not met under the baseline MS sales plan. Management is actively working three tracks in priority order: FE block sale (primary — monetizes existing asset), surplus note issuance (~$150M via RBC Capital Markets), and quota share reinsurance on MS new sales (GenRe). Nothing is close to execution yet. The RBC KPI is expected to move green as a solution reaches certainty.

Capital Playbook: Exists but does not yet specify which options are preferred under varying circumstances. Needs further work before it is a reliable decision tool.
What's Next
Advance capital solution tracks toward term sheets. Complete Capital Opportunities Playbook with circumstance-specific recommendations. Share CapCo capital scenario modeling.
