Digital-First Strategy

Technology never happens for the sake of technology alone. Digital is the delivery mechanism for everything else we are trying to accomplish.

Digital Enabler → Strategic Objective → Execution / Outcome → KPI → Owner → Status → Signal

Strategic Intent

Why This Enabler Exists

The 2023 strategy called it "operational rationalization." The 2025 strategy renames it Digital-First — and the shift matters. Operational rationalization was defensive: do more with less. Digital-First is offensive: embed data, AI, and digital capabilities into the fabric of every function to enable real-time decision-making, seamless experiences, and scalable innovation.

The vision: all applications submitted electronically at point of sale. All phone calls self-service or automated. All post-issue transactions self-service. 100% auto-adjudication of claims. Every agent transaction automated. This is not a technology roadmap — it is a picture of the company we are building.

What It Is Meant To Solve

  • →Manual processing that limits scale — more volume without proportional headcount
  • →Data siloed across legacy systems — no 360° view of customer or agent
  • →Agent and customer experiences that lag modern expectations
  • →AI capability that is still in "experimentation" rather than embedded in core processes

Key Tradeoffs

  • →Moving from agent-first to dual-focus (agent + consumer) without alienating either
  • →Speed of AI adoption vs. governance rigor — neither can be sacrificed
  • →Talent: significant demographic shift underway; 11% of employees reach retirement age by 2027

What We Committed To

Four Digital Dimensions

1 · Customer & Agent Centric

Drive engagement that anticipates needs, not just responds to them.

AI-enabled support. Personalized omnichannel journeys. Voice-of-customer feedback loops. Unified agent tools with full customer context. Agent Portal MVP by June 2026. Customer Portal rewrite underway. Payment and Claims journey automation in H1 2026.

2 · Data-Driven Decision Making

Every decision empowered by trusted data and responsible AI.

Data Lakehouse housing 80% of enterprise data by 2026. Enterprise Data Quality Score 95 (target >90). Customer 360 and Agent 360 views. Real-time analytics and AI-powered dashboards. 70% user adoption of data-based decisioning by 2028.

3 · Digital Capabilities

Operational excellence through automation and AI at scale.

Straight-through processing as the default, not the exception. Auto-adjudication scaled. Cloud-native, API-first architecture. AI governance framework in place. 333 of 374 employees actively using Copilot today — 20,000 prompts in last 30 days.

4 · Talent Evolution

Redesigning skills for the future of work.

Four generations currently employed, each bringing different technology fluency. Leadership count decreasing; strategic roles increasing. AI literacy and Copilot adoption tracked as a KPI. Succession planning with ready-soon successors for all ELT roles. Internal mobility as a deliberate tool.

Leadership Signals

What This Enabler Helps Leaders Answer

Questions This Enabler Answers

  • Are we automating fast enough to keep pace with volume growth without adding proportional headcount?
  • Is our data infrastructure trustworthy enough to make AI-enabled decisions reliable?
  • Are agents and customers experiencing tangibly better interactions quarter over quarter?
  • Is AI moving from experimental to embedded — with governance in place?
  • Do we have the right talent composition to execute the next phase?

What Yellow/Red Signals Mean

Yellow on New Business automation (current) means health volume complexity is outpacing automation readiness. Projects are in flight; resolution expected by AEP. No action required unless timeline slips.

Yellow on data quality or lakehouse migration would be a foundational signal — AI and analytics built on bad data are worse than no analytics.

Red on talent KPIs would mean the strategy is at risk at the execution layer, not the technology layer.

What Is Intentionally Not Optimized Here

Digital does not optimize for technology adoption for its own sake. Copilot usage is tracked not because prompts are valuable — but because AI literacy is an organizational capability that compounds. Tools without literacy do nothing.
