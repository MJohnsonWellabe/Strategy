Capital Strategy

Proactive capital management is not a finance function. It is a strategic function. Without sufficient capital, every growth and digital commitment is contingent.

Strategic Intent → What We Committed To → Capital Levers → Leadership Signals → KPIs

Strategic Intent

Why This Enabler Exists

🔴

Active Red KPI — RBC Trajectory

RBC is projected to decline well below the 450% target — reaching approximately 350% at the mid-period low point under expected sales levels. Management is actively pursuing an FE block sale (primary track), a surplus note (~$100M), and quota share reinsurance on MS new sales. Nothing is close to execution. The KPI is expected to move green as a capital solution reaches certainty of execution.

Capital is the permission structure for everything else. Medicare Supplement expansion requires over $150M in capital support for new sales between 2025 and 2030. RBC is projected to decline from 735% at YE24 before recovering in the early 2030s. The path from here to there requires proactive management — not just monitoring.

The Capital strategy does three things: builds a transparent framework for allocation decisions, develops the modeling capability to forecast and scenario-plan with confidence, and executes capital solutions that preserve the financial resilience to fund the strategy without compromising policyholder protection.

What It Is Meant To Solve

  • →RBC pressure from Medicare Supplement growth consuming capital faster than earnings generate it through 2030
  • →Capital allocation decisions made without a transparent, agreed-upon framework
  • →Actuarial and financial modeling that hasn't kept pace with the complexity of strategic decisions being asked of it

Key Tradeoffs

  • →Reinsurance reduces required capital but cedes a share of returns and some strategic control
  • →Surplus notes raise capital with interest cost, reducing net returns on backed products
  • →Slowing MS sales growth protects RBC but destroys long-term embedded value and market position

What We Committed To

Capital Commitments

Capital Framework

A single, transparent framework for every major capital deployment decision.

IRR, VNB, capital intensity, risk profile, and strategic fit — evaluated consistently. Eliminates ad hoc capital conversations by making the decision criteria visible and agreed-upon in advance. MVP developed and used for 26R2 plan.

Actuarial & Financial Modeling

Modeling capability that earns leadership's trust and drives decisions.

The goal is not a single accuracy metric — it is a modeling environment where CapCo can get credible answers to difficult capital questions quickly. Capital scenario modeling completed in Q1. Liquidity/cash flow model progressing. Leadership should be able to rely on the model, not second-guess it.

RBC Management

Projected RBC above 450% through 2026–2028. Currently red.

FE block sale is the primary capital track (monetizes an existing asset). Surplus note issuance (~$100M, RBC Capital Markets) is the second track. Quota share reinsurance on MS new sales is the third. Management is working toward execution certainty on all three.

Capital Opportunities Playbook

A documented decision menu by circumstance. Currently red.

Playbook exists but doesn't yet specify which options are preferred under which scenarios. This is decision architecture — it means leadership never starts from scratch when capital pressure emerges again.

Capital Allocation Governance

CapCo operating as an integrated decision system.

Capital Planning, Allocation, Risk Management, and Performance Monitoring as one connected system — not separate conversations. Transparent with regulators, rating agencies, and the board.

Strategic Context

We consume ~$290M of capital 2025–2028. We recover.

After-tax earnings on inforce, institutional lines, and new business recoveries make Wellabe capital-accretive again by 2032–2035. The plan is credible under 30+ stress scenarios. The challenge is execution, not viability.

How This Shows Up Operationally

Capital Solutions — In Priority Order

Three active tracks, sequenced by proximity to execution and strategic preference.

Track 1 — Primary

FE Block Sale

Monetizes the Final Expense block — an asset already on the books. RBC Capital Markets engaged for valuation. Proceeds generate capital without diluting holding company ownership or incurring ongoing interest cost. Closest to execution certainty of the three tracks.

Track 2

Surplus Note (~$100M)

Preferred over traditional debt — viewed more favorably by regulators and rating agencies. Maturity and face value flexibility. Currently in active discussion with RBC Capital Markets. Requires regulatory approval. Adds interest cost but preserves full ownership and operational control.

Track 3

Quota Share Reinsurance on MS New Sales

Cedes a portion of Medicare Supplement new business to a reinsurer, transferring new business strain in exchange for a share of future returns. Reduces ongoing capital consumption from growth. Active discussions with GenRe as an amendment to the existing treaty. Requires regulatory approval.

Leadership Signals

What This Enabler Helps Leaders Answer

Questions This Enabler Answers

  • Can we sustain the current MS sales plan without breaching our RBC floor?
  • Which capital solution track is closest to execution — and what is the hold-up?
  • Under which scenarios would we need to reduce MS sales targets to preserve RBC?
  • Is our financial modeling capable enough to support real-time capital decision making?

What Red Signals Mean Here

Red on RBC trajectory is the most consequential metric on this site. It does not mean we are in crisis — we are not. It means the growth plan, executed as modeled, creates a capital gap that requires an external solution. The management team is working that problem. Leadership and the board should expect quarterly updates on execution certainty of the three capital tracks.

Red on the Capital Playbook is a governance risk. Without it, the next capital decision defaults to re-deliberation from scratch rather than a structured evaluation.

What Is Intentionally Not Optimized Here

Capital does not optimize for maximum RBC at all times. A very high RBC ratio in a growth-oriented mutual insurer often signals under-deployment — capital sitting idle rather than funding policyholder value. The target range exists for a reason.
