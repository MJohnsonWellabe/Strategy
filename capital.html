Capital Strategy

Proactive capital management is not a finance function. It is a strategic function. Without sufficient capital, every growth and digital commitment is contingent.

Capital Enabler → Strategic Objective → Execution / Outcome → KPI → Owner → Status → Signal

Strategic Intent

Why This Enabler Exists

🔴

Active Red KPI — RBC Trajectory

Projected RBC declines to approximately 350% in 2028 at expected sales levels — below the 450% target. Management is actively pursuing surplus notes, FE block reinsurance, structured reinsurance (Canada Life), and Gen Re coinsurance. Nothing is close to execution. The metric is expected to go green as a capital solution reaches greater certainty.

Capital is the permission structure for everything else. Medicare Supplement expansion requires over $150M in capital support for new sales between 2025 and 2030. RBC is projected to decline from 735% at YE24 to approximately 450% in 2031 before recovering. The path from here to there requires proactive management — not just monitoring.

The Capital strategy does three things: it builds a transparent framework for capital allocation decisions (so we stop relitigating the same questions), it develops the tools to forecast capital with confidence, and it maintains the financial resilience to fund the strategy without compromising policyholder protection.

What It Is Meant To Solve

  • →RBC pressure from Medicare Supplement and annuity growth consuming capital faster than earnings generate it (2025–2030)
  • →Capital allocation decisions made without a transparent, agreed-upon framework
  • →Actuarial modeling that lacks the agility to support real-time capital decisions

Key Tradeoffs

  • →Reinsurance reduces required capital but gives up a share of returns and some strategic control
  • →Surplus notes raise capital with interest cost, reducing product returns on backed capital
  • →Slowing MS sales growth protects RBC but destroys long-term embedded value and market position

What We Committed To

Five Capital Commitments

Capital Framework

A single, transparent framework for every major capital deployment decision.

IRR, VNB, capital intensity, risk profile, and strategic fit — evaluated consistently. Eliminates "food fight" capital conversations by making the decision criteria visible and agreed-upon in advance. MVP developed and used for 26R2 plan.

Actuarial Modeling

RBC estimates within 3% of actuals. Capital scenarios delivered with agility.

Pre-covariance RBC calculation within tolerance (Q1 2026). Capital scenario modeling completed and shared with CapCo. Liquidity/cash flow model progressing. The goal: CapCo recommendations that leadership trusts.

RBC Management

Projected RBC above 450% through 2026–2028. Currently red.

Multiple tracks active: C-2 financing reinsurance (Hannover/GenRe), quota share on MS new sales (GenRe), surplus note issuance (~$100M via RBC Capital Markets), Agency valuation (ARIS). Management is working toward certainty of execution.

Capital Opportunities Playbook

A documented menu of capital actions by circumstance. Currently red.

Playbook exists but doesn't yet specify which options are preferred under which scenarios. This is not just documentation — it is decision architecture. Completing it means leadership never starts from scratch when capital pressure emerges.

Capital Allocation Governance

CapCo operating with confidence in the models that support it.

Capital Planning, Capital Allocation, Risk Management, and Performance Monitoring as an integrated system — not separate conversations. Transparency with regulators, rating agencies, and the board.

Strategic Context

We consume $290M of capital 2025–2028. We recover.

After-tax earnings on inforce, institutional lines, and new business recoveries make Wellabe capital-accretive again by 2032–2035. The plan is credible under 30+ stress scenarios. The question is execution, not viability.

How This Shows Up Operationally

Capital Management Levers

Debt

Surplus notes as the primary tool.

Preferred over traditional debt — viewed more favorably by regulators and rating agencies. Maturity and face value flexibility. ~$100M issuance currently in discussion with RBC Capital Markets. Requires regulatory approval.

Reinsurance

Highest Priority

C-2 financing + quota share on MS block.

C-2 financing reduces required capital while retaining full block returns (minus ~40–80bps financing fee). Quota share transfers new business strain. Both in active discussions with Hannover, GenRe, Canada Life, and Aspida.

Equity / Agency

ARIS Agency valuation underway.

RBC Capital Markets engaged to value ARIS. Outcome could be sale, borrowing against asset, or bringing in third-party equity owners. Monetizes an asset not currently on the balance sheet. No decision made; valuation first.

Six KPIs. Four green. Two red — the RBC trajectory and the Capital Opportunities Playbook. Both have named owners and active management plans. Red here is not a surprise; it is a documented risk with a defined response.

Leadership Signals

What This Enabler Helps Leaders Answer

Questions This Enabler Answers

  • Can we sustain the current MS sales plan without breaching our RBC floor?
  • Which capital solution should we execute first, given lead times and regulatory requirements?
  • Under which scenarios would we need to reduce MS sales targets to preserve RBC?
  • Is our capital forecasting model reliable enough to commit to strategic decisions?

What Red Signals Mean Here

Red on RBC trajectory is the most consequential metric on this site. It does not mean we are in crisis — we are not. It means the growth plan, executed as modeled, creates a capital gap that requires an external solution. The management team is working that problem. Leadership and the board should expect quarterly updates on execution certainty of the capital tracks in motion.

Red on the Capital Playbook is a governance risk, not an operational risk. If the playbook is not completed, the next capital decision defaults to re-deliberation from scratch.

What Is Intentionally Not Optimized Here

Capital does not optimize for maximum RBC at all times. A very high RBC ratio in a growth-oriented mutual insurer often means under-deployment — leaving policyholder value on the table. The target range exists for a reason.
